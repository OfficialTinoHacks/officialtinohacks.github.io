WHO ARE WE?

TWO DAYS OF INNOVATION.

Since its inception in 2016, TinoHacks has grown to one of the largest and oldest high school hackathons in the Bay Area. We aim to provide students with a brilliant community to develop their entrepreneurial and coding expertise. TinoHacks III is a 24-hour hackathon where students of all experience levels are encouraged to design and build innovative projects in all immersive environment.

PLAN

Students are encouraged to identify a problem they would like to solve, before the hackathon.

COLLABORATE

In teams, collaboration is key to a succesful project and valuable learning experience.

INNOVATE

Innovation at TinoHacks is limited to the partcipant's creativity and imagination.

AN ALL INCLUSIVE ENVIRONMENT

TinoHacks is open to everyone and we mean it. We strive to create a safe all inclusive community for all participants. At TinoHacks we ensure that all participants are feel respected and included regadless of their identity.


INSPIRING A FUTURE GENERATION

Some of Silicon Valley's most talented and ingenious students will gather and put their heads together to create pioneering solutions to challenges that exist in our world today. Tinohacks III aims to provide and nurture students with a brilliant environment to develop their entrepreneurial and coding expertise.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS



What is TinoHacks?


TinoHacks III is a two day long hackathon hosted by Cupertino High School students where both new and experienced programmers can work together on projects, learn new skills, and have a splendid time. TinoHacks I happened on April 15-16, 2017, and TinoHacks II took place on April 21-22, 2018! Our third event is coming back in April 2019. backed by HackClub, a 501 (c)(3) public charity registered in CA. All donations are tax - deductible. Please feel free to email us at hello@tinohacks.tech if you have any questions or concerns.

What is a hackathon?

A Hackathon is a 24 to 36 hour long coding marathon event where people can make the most bizarre ideas into reality. Over the course of the weekend, students collaborate to create projects, listen to exquisite workshop presentations, exercise their programming abilities, and of course, have tons of fun!

Time and location?

TinoHacks III will be taking place on the weekend of April 20-21, 2019. Check back later for details of event location!

Do I need programming experience?

No! In fact, TinoHacks is a great way to begin your programming experience! A basic understanding is recommended, but we will have workshops and mentors to help you make your project idea a reality.

What will I need?

You will need a laptop, a valid student ID (or some sort of student ID), chargers, your phone, a blanket/sleeping bag, toiletries, and any supplies you need for your hack.

Who can attend?

This event is open to high schoolers ONLY. No middle schoolers will be allowed. However, college students may sign up to mentor, volunteer, or lead a workshop. Sign up here, or email hello@tinohacks.tech if you have any questions or concerns.

Do I need to stay overnight?

No; you will be permitted to go home at a certain time, though staying the night is highly recommended. The doors will close at 7pm and open at 7am, and no persons are allowed to leave or enter the building during that time.

Team requirements?

You need at least 1 person per team and 4 people at most.

Travel reimbursements?

Unfortunately, TinoHacks will not be offering travel reimbursements. Please email us at hello@tinohacks.tech for additional details.
